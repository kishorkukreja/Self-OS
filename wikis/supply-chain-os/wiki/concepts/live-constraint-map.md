# Live Constraint Map

**Definition:** A live constraint map names the current binding supply-chain constraint, assigns ownership, and links it to decisions about buys, buffers, routes, pricing, and supplier actions.

**Why it matters:** The 2026-05-08 daily research note argues that static risk registers miss the speed at which constraints move between tariffs, energy, food, supplier delivery, AI infrastructure, power equipment, and logistics capacity. A live map turns volatility into an operating process.
