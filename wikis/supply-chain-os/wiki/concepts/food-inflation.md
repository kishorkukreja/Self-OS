# Food Inflation as Supply-Chain Signal

**Definition:** Food inflation becomes a supply-chain signal when price movement reflects physical constraints such as energy, fertilizer, weather, freight routes, and commodity availability.

**Why it matters:** The 2026-05-08 research uses FAO's April Food Price Index rise to connect vegetable oils, meat, cereals, fertilizer affordability, energy costs, and Strait of Hormuz disruption. Operators should read food prices as part of a broader landed-cost and supplier-risk picture.
