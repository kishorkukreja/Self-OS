# Constraint Migration

**Definition:** Constraint migration describes supply-chain stress that changes location faster than static planning models can respond.

**Why it matters:** The daily research note uses PMI, tariff, packaging, data-center, and trade signals to show that the constraint can move from tariffs to routing, from fuel to supplier lead times, from chips to transformers, and from sourcing alternatives to qualification capacity.

## 2026-05-09 — Live maps and May 8 signals

The May 8 research adds a sharper operating frame: constraints moved across food prices, energy, fertilizer, manufacturing inputs, services delivery, AI infrastructure, and logistics platform strategy inside the same week. The recommended response is a live constraint map that names the current bottleneck and changes buying, buffering, routing, or pricing decisions accordingly.

## 2026-05-11 — W19 weekly issue synthesis

The 2026-W19 weekly package adds more evidence that the binding constraint moved across manufacturing internals, tariff deadlines, food-price pressure, freight capacity, and AI-infrastructure bottlenecks. The issue frames constraint migration as an operating cadence problem: monthly S&OP, static risk registers, and one-off tariff memos cannot respond quickly enough when cost, lead time, supplier qualification, customer promise, and cash exposure shift within the same week.
