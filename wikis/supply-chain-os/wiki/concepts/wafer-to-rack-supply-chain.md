# Wafer to Rack Supply Chain

**Definition:** The end-to-end physical chain that turns semiconductor capacity, memory, power, cooling, networking, logistics, and integration into deployable AI racks.

## Update — 2026-05-02

[[sources/supply-chain-signals-2026-05-01-daily-research]] expands Wafer to Rack from a semiconductor-chain story into a full physical-constraint map for AI capacity. The bottlenecks include specialty gases, copper, aluminum, HBM, advanced packaging, server integration, cooling, power equipment, grid interconnection, construction labor, and commissioning. The useful operating principle: deployable AI compute is governed by the slowest physical constraint, not only by GPU allocation.
