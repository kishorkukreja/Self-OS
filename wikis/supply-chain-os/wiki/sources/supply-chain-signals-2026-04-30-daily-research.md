## Summary

The 2026-04-30 Supply Chain Signals daily research file turns the source registry into editorial candidates and operator questions. The dominant theme is [[concepts/freight-market-disruption]] caused by Middle East conflict, port congestion, air-capacity constraints, unusual routings, fuel pressure, and compliance changes. The research frames disruption as a multi-layer network problem rather than a single bottleneck: lane-specific lead-time variance, domestic freight cost pressure, inventory uncertainty, and customs data quality all interact.

The file also identifies two candidate chain narratives. "Wafer to Rack" remains the lead Chain of the Week because AI infrastructure demand depends on semiconductors, HBM, advanced packaging, copper, critical minerals, power/cooling equipment, rack integration, logistics, and tariff documentation. "Farm to Fork" is a backup if food input inflation becomes the stronger weekly theme. The advantage section argues that decision-grade visibility, agentic procurement/compliance workflows, and pre-modeled mode-switching options can turn supply-chain operations from a cost center into a competitive asset. This is a working research artifact for newsletter production, not a client-confidential deliverable.

## Key takeaways
- The daily research frames Middle East logistics disruption as a stacked network problem spanning ocean, air, ports, domestic freight, fuel, inventory, and compliance.
- Wafer to Rack is the lead chain candidate because AI infrastructure demand depends on scarce physical components, power/cooling equipment, logistics, and traceability.
- Operator questions focus on lane-specific promise risk, strategic safety stock versus nervous pull-forward, and ownership of trade-compliance/refund data quality.
