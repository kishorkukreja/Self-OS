# Tompkins Solutions: Designing Omnichannel Distribution Centers

**Type:** industry-report  
**Date:** 2026-04-26  
**Client:** internal  
**Confidential:** false  
**Raw file:** [[../raw/industry-reports/2026-04-26-tompkins-omnichannel-distribution-centers.md]]

**Summary:** [[entities/tompkins-solutions.md|Tompkins Solutions]] argues that tomorrow’s retailers must fulfil from anywhere, ship to anywhere, and handle returns efficiently from one integrated hub. The article proposes five design elements for [[concepts/omnichannel-fulfillment.md|omnichannel distribution centres]]: flexible modular layouts, integrated warehouse systems (WMS/OMS/TMS), adaptive automation and robotics, efficient returns processing, and real-time data visibility via IoT and RFID.

**Key takeaways:**
- Modular zones can expand or contract for single-unit picks vs. bulk pallet handling.
- Integrated tech stacks provide full inventory visibility across stores, DCs, and suppliers.
- Returns must be processed inside the same facility rather than outsourced.
- DCs should be viewed as strategic nerve centres of customer engagement, not just storage.
