# McKinsey: Creating a Consumer-Centric Supply Chain Strategy

**Type:** industry-report  
**Date:** 2026-04-26  
**Client:** internal  
**Confidential:** false  
**Raw file:** [[../raw/industry-reports/2026-04-26-mckinsey-consumer-centric-supply-chain-strategy.md]]

**Summary:** [[entities/mckinsey.md|McKinsey]] argues that a one-size-fits-all supply chain is obsolete in an omnichannel world. Winning requires a three-step, consumer-back approach: define segments by consumer requirements, product characteristics, and supply-chain structure; articulate a “North Star” across four differentiation dimensions (service, availability, cost, sustainability); and build the network, planning, and fulfilment capabilities to deliver it.

**Key takeaways:**
- Segmentation must include brick-and-mortar stores, wholesale partners, and marketplaces.
- Service differentiation spans speed, optionality/flexibility, and convenience.
- Network design should place the right nodes (warehouses, stores, dark stores) in the right locations for a multi-speed supply chain.
- Geospatial demand data should drive SKU placement rather than stocking every SKU everywhere.
