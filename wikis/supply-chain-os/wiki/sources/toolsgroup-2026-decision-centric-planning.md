# ToolsGroup: Decision-Centric Supply Chain Planning Outlook 2026

**Type:** industry-report  
**Date:** 2026-04-26  
**Client:** internal  
**Confidential:** false  
**Raw file:** [[../raw/industry-reports/2026-04-26-toolsgroup-decision-centric-planning-2026.md]]

**Summary:** Based on the 2026 Gartner Magic Quadrant for Supply Chain Planning Solutions, [[entities/toolsgroup.md|ToolsGroup]] describes a paradigm shift from periodic batch planning to continuous, adaptive decision systems. Planning becomes a "decision repository" where forecasts are inputs to broader trade-off questions. The article reframes inventory as a network-level decision problem and treats [[concepts/segmentation.md|segmentation]] as a living framework rather than a one-time exercise.

**Key takeaways:**
- Competitive advantage comes from superior decision-making when plans deviate, not from perfect forecasts.
- Inventory and service are not fixed trade-offs; excess inventory and poor service often coexist because of wrong stock positioning.
- Scenario-driven planning should be part of regular operations, not just annual strategy.
- AI amplifies planning through pattern recognition, large-scale trade-off exploration, and prescriptive recommendations.
