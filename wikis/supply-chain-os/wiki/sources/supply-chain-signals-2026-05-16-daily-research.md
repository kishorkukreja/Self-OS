## Summary

This daily research pack frames the week’s supply-chain signals around conditional policy relief and constraint migration. U.S.–China trade talks are treated as a possible managed basket of non-sensitive goods, not a broad tariff reset. That means sourcing teams still need SKU-level classification, country-of-origin evidence, supplier alternatives, and substitution economics. The pharma tariff story makes a similar point: threatened branded-drug tariffs are already changing behavior before enforcement through U.S. manufacturing investments, stockpiling, pricing commitments, and platform positioning, but the real bottlenecks are validation, quality release, tech transfer, and supplier qualification.

The pack also separates inflation and capacity signals that can otherwise get blurred. FAO food data points to vegetable oils and meat moving differently from sugar and dairy. ISM services data shows expansion with slower supplier deliveries and elevated prices. AI infrastructure remains the strongest chain-of-the-week candidate, but the analysis broadens the constraint beyond chips into HBM, packaging, power equipment, copper, cooling, networking, server assembly, logistics, and data-center commissioning. Logistics AI appears as a vendor-selection criterion, but the pack stresses ROI discipline: buyers should ask which exception is resolved faster or which handoff disappears.

## Key takeaways

- Treat tariff relief as conditional SKU-level optionality rather than a clean policy reset.
- Model localization projects through validation and qualification calendars, not only capacity announcements.
- Analyze AI infrastructure as a wafer-to-rack dependency stack with multiple non-chip constraints.
- Demand operational ROI evidence from logistics AI vendors before scaling pilots.
