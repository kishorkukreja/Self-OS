## Summary

This daily research file turns the 2026-05-01 source registry into editorial candidates and operator questions. It identifies six top moves: faster-than-expected CBP tariff refunds through CAPE, UP/Norfolk Southern's amended merger filing, freight cost control shifting from rates to contract rules, AI infrastructure scarcity beyond chips, energy shock spilling into commodities/trade, and food input costs rising through the energy channel. The recurring method is to translate headlines into operating questions for shippers, procurement teams, and supply-chain leaders.

The file's strongest strategic concept is the Wafer to Rack chain. It argues that AI capacity is allocated by the slowest physical constraint, not by the fastest chip designer: specialty gases, copper, aluminum, wafer fabrication, advanced packaging, HBM, server integration, power equipment, cooling, construction, interconnection, and commissioning all matter. Secondary themes include continuous supplier monitoring, freight audit/payment as a control layer, and AI demand sensing with readiness gates. The source is non-confidential and can safely feed concepts and entities.
