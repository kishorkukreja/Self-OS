# AI in the Chain: AI-Driven Demand Sensing Lessons from Unilever and Amazon

**Type:** article  
**Date:** 2026-04-26  
**Client:** internal  
**Confidential:** false  
**Raw file:** [[../raw/articles/2026-04-26-ai-in-the-chain-ai-demand-sensing-unilever-amazon.md]]

**Summary:** The article defines [[concepts/demand-sensing.md|AI-driven demand sensing]] as near-term demand prediction using real-time signals such as POS, weather, social media, and market events. It contrasts this with traditional 60–70% accuracy rates and highlights results from [[entities/unilever.md|Unilever]] (30% forecast-error reduction, ~$300M saved) and [[entities/amazon.md|Amazon]] (~$1B inventory savings, 50% picking-efficiency gain). A five-step adoption roadmap is provided.

**Key takeaways:**
- Demand sensing uses ensemble ML (gradient-boosted trees, random forests, neural networks) and reinforcement learning.
- Implementation barriers include data integration, data quality, algorithm bias, and functional silos.
- Quick wins include lower working capital, fewer stockouts, and automated deviation alerts.
- Cultural resistance from planners accustomed to monthly cycles is a significant change-management hurdle.
