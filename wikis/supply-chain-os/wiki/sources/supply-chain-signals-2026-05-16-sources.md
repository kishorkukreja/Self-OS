## Summary

This source registry records the evidence base behind the 2026-05-16 Supply Chain Signals research pack. Reuters provides the managed U.S.–China tariff-basket signal and the pharma localization/tariff response story. FAO and Reuters supply the food-price evidence, with FAO as the primary quantitative source for the April 2026 Food Price Index and component moves. ISM contributes services-sector diffusion data, especially supplier deliveries and prices. Manufacturing Dive/Omdia gives the qualitative AI-infrastructure dependency-stack framing. BCG/Alpega, Project44, and FourKites provide logistics-AI adoption and value-realization signals, while FreightWaves/Global Port Tracker supplies port-to-shelf context around container imports, pull-forward inventory, and tariff uncertainty.

The registry is useful because it distinguishes hard quantitative sources from interpretive or vendor-positioning sources. FAO and ISM are appropriate for charts and metrics cards. Reuters is appropriate for timely policy and company-behavior facts, with care around proposed rather than implemented trade mechanisms. Manufacturing Dive/Omdia is better for systems diagrams than unsupported numerical claims. Vendor and consulting sources are useful for market language and buyer expectations, but they should be checked against measurable operational outcomes before being used as proof of value.

## Key takeaways

- Separates primary data sources from narrative, opinion, consulting, and vendor sources.
- Preserves quantitative caveats for tariffs, food indexes, services PMI, and logistics AI adoption.
- Supports future Sunday-brief synthesis around Wafer to Rack, Molecule to Medicine, Farm to Fork, and Port to Shelf chains.
