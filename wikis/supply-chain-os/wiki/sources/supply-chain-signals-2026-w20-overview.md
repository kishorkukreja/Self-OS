## Summary

The 2026-W20 overview sets the editorial frame for the Supply Chain Signals weekly research folder. It identifies Wafer to Rack as the starting chain of the week while leaving room to pivot if stronger evidence emerges. The thesis links U.S. manufacturing expansion, rising prices, slower supplier deliveries, tariff uncertainty, uneven ocean demand, and the operationalization of AI/robotics into a single planning problem. The file is not a final newsletter; it is a working map that tells later research how to organize daily notes, source maps, briefs, and visuals. Its value in the wiki is to preserve the initial hypothesis before the Sunday synthesis is written, so future readers can see how the week’s constraint narrative formed and whether later evidence confirmed or changed it. The source is useful primarily as a compiled signal rather than as a final answer: the raw capture remains the canonical place for exact wording, examples, figures, and links.
