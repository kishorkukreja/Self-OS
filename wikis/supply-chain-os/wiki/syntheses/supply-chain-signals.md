## What this pipeline does

Supply Chain Signals is the weekly newsletter pipeline for tracking the bottlenecks, breakthroughs, and hidden systems shaping global supply chains. It collects daily research during the week and turns the strongest recurring patterns into a Sunday publishing package.

The source of truth for each issue is the weekly raw folder:

- Current week: `wikis/supply-chain-os/raw/newsletters/supply-chain-signals/2026-W19/`
- Root folder: `wikis/supply-chain-os/raw/newsletters/supply-chain-signals/`

Each weekly folder contains:

- `daily/` — daily research captures
- `sources/` — source lists and extraction notes
- `brief/` — Sunday publishing drafts for Substack, LinkedIn, X/thread, carousel outline, PPT/consulting-style outline, and visuals plan

## Current editorial defaults

- First Chain of the Week: Wafer to Rack
- Source mix: executive sources, operator sources, research sources, and tech/systems sources
- Voice: clear, specific, humanized, operator-useful, not AI-sounding
- Output formats: Substack draft, LinkedIn posts, X/thread, carousel outline, PPT/consulting-style outline, visuals plan

## Current issue angle

The 2026-W19 draft argues that the week’s strongest signal is constraint migration. Tariffs, manufacturing input costs, supplier-delivery delays, food-price pressure, freight fuel/routing risk, and AI infrastructure bottlenecks all point to the same operating problem: companies need a live view of the currently binding constraint and the authority to change the plan.
