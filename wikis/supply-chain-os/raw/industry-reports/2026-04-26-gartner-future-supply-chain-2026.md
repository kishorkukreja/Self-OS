# Future of Supply Chains 2026: Ensure Your Supply Chains are Future-Fit

**Source:** [Gartner](https://www.gartner.com/en/supply-chain/topics/future-of-supply-chain)

---

## Executive Snapshot

> **81% of supply chain leaders** embracing AI feel confident in managing its impact, **while only 54%** of the rest of respondents report the same.

The future of supply chain is defined by a shift from operational excellence to **commercial innovation**. CSCOs must leverage AI, talent transformation, and machine-enabled collaboration across **five strategic pillars** while navigating **three major challenges** to remain future-fit.

---

## 3 Critical Challenges for CSCOs

### 1. CEO Pressure: Prove Growth Partnership
When CSCOs focus primarily on cost control, CEOs perceive the supply chain as failing to support business growth—eroding the CSCO’s standing as a high-value strategic partner.

**Invest in capabilities that drive commercial growth, even when outcomes are hard to measure:**
- Collaborative, trust-based relationships with key suppliers
- Strategy and policies for supply chain risk mitigation
- A social learning environment that fosters knowledge exchange
- Dedicated resources to design workforce strategies for future talent
- Customer satisfaction data capture, use, and protection
- Sustainable materials and packaging

### 2. Low Productivity Gains on GenAI Investments
> GenAI is deployed by a reported **72% of supply chain organizations**—but most are experiencing middling results for productivity and ROI.

**Actionable insight:** Simply adjusting AI investment up or down is not the answer. CSCOs must redefine value by investing in **intangible assets**: the employees who provide data analysis and strategic direction, and the ecosystems that support production. Maximizing digital tool productivity depends on these human and ecosystem enablers.

### 3. Economic Volatility
Increasing regulation and geopolitical tensions are driving long-term uncertainty.

**Response:** Tackle root causes (not symptoms) by:
- Increasing geopolitical visibility
- Leveraging scenario analysis
- Partnering within the supply chain ecosystem to mitigate disruption

## The 5 Strategic Pillars

### 1. Supply Chain Vision
A clear leadership vision is essential to navigate volatility and act decisively. High-performing organizations continue accelerating investment in future capabilities despite risk and uncertain returns.

### 2. Real-Time Execution (Beyond Analytics)
> **95%** of supply chains must quickly react to change, but **only 7%** can execute decisions in real time.

Without real-time execution, organizations know what to do but cannot act fast enough. **Digital supply chain investment in real-time decision execution will increase 5x by 2028.**

**Three-step transformation:**
1. Access to real-time data from across the end-to-end supply chain
2. Capability to analyze that data on the fly, enabling real-time decision making
3. Real-time decision making that triggers an expectation for real-time execution

**Enablers:** Overcome legacy technology and data gaps by deploying AI, large language models (LLMs), augmented reality (AR)/virtual reality (VR), and other technologies that enable rather than overwhelm.

### 3. CX for Supply Chain
Improving customer experience (CX) is a **top 3 business priority**. Supply chain performance is a key driver of repurchase behavior.

> **Enabled customers are 2x as likely to repurchase**, but **only 23%** of supply chains focus on enabling their customers.

Many organizations prioritize operational efficiency and OTIF (on time, in full) over reducing customer pain points.

**Key actions to scale CX globally:**
- Gather **voice of the customer (VoC)** data to inform personas and journey maps that reduce customer and employee effort
- **Streamline the supply chain service menu**, offering capabilities "**as a service**" and differentiating the experience order-to-order across the buying journey
- Gain funding via **cost-to-serve transparency** to address high service delivery costs
- Accelerate the pace of launching new services

### 4. Human-Centric Work & EVP
> Over the next three to five years, **63% of supply chain organizations** expect the high competition for talent to continue.

Only a minority anticipate increased talent availability from historically underrepresented regions (Africa, Middle East, Eastern Europe) or from AI deployments alone.

To attract, retain, and engage future-ready talent, CSCOs must offer an **Employee Value Proposition (EVP)** that moves beyond financial motivation:
- Flexible, purposeful day-to-day experiences
- Environments where employees feel understood, autonomous, valued, invested, and cared for
- Pathways for personal development and **choice in work**

**Competitive advantage** stems from attracting the right skills *and* maintaining higher employee engagement to drive organizational success.

### 5. Sustainable Supply Chain
> **67% of supply chains** have formal objectives to increase sustainability, but many struggle to translate goals into operational reality.

Sustainability must be embedded into network design, sourcing decisions, and product lifecycle management rather than treated as a standalone initiative.
