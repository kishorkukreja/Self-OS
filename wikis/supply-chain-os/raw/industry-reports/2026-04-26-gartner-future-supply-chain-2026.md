    10|# Future of Supply Chains 2026: Ensure Your Supply Chains are Future-Fit
    11|
    12|**Source:** [Gartner](https://www.gartner.com/en/supply-chain/topics/future-of-supply-chain)
    13|
    14|---
    15|
    16|## Executive Snapshot
    17|
    18|> **81% of supply chain leaders** embracing AI feel confident in managing its impact, **while only 54%** of the rest of respondents report the same.
    19|
    20|The future of supply chain is defined by a shift from operational excellence to **commercial innovation**. CSCOs must leverage AI, talent transformation, and machine-enabled collaboration across **five strategic pillars** while navigating **three major challenges** to remain future-fit.
    21|
    22|---
    23|
    24|## 3 Critical Challenges for CSCOs
    25|
    26|### 1. CEO Pressure: Prove Growth Partnership
    27|When CSCOs focus primarily on cost control, CEOs perceive the supply chain as failing to support business growth—eroding the CSCO’s standing as a high-value strategic partner.
    28|
    29|**Invest in capabilities that drive commercial growth, even when outcomes are hard to measure:**
    30|- Collaborative, trust-based relationships with key suppliers
    31|- Strategy and policies for supply chain risk mitigation
    32|- A social learning environment that fosters knowledge exchange
    33|- Dedicated resources to design workforce strategies for future talent
    34|- Customer satisfaction data capture, use, and protection
    35|- Sustainable materials and packaging
    36|
    37|### 2. Low Productivity Gains on GenAI Investments
    38|> GenAI is deployed by a reported **72% of supply chain organizations**—but most are experiencing middling results for productivity and ROI.
    39|
    40|**Actionable insight:** Simply adjusting AI investment up or down is not the answer. CSCOs must redefine value by investing in **intangible assets**: the employees who provide data analysis and strategic direction, and the ecosystems that support production. Maximizing digital tool productivity depends on these human and ecosystem enablers.
    41|
    42|### 3. Economic Volatility
    43|Increasing regulation and geopolitical tensions are driving long-term uncertainty.
    44|
    45|**Response:** Tackle root causes (not symptoms) by:
    46|- Increasing geopolitical visibility
    47|- Leveraging scenario analysis
    48|- Partnering within the supply chain ecosystem to mitigate disruption
    49|

    51|
    52|## The 5 Strategic Pillars
    53|
    54|### 1. Supply Chain Vision
    55|A clear leadership vision is essential to navigate volatility and act decisively. High-performing organizations continue accelerating investment in future capabilities despite risk and uncertain returns.
    56|
    57|### 2. Real-Time Execution (Beyond Analytics)
    58|> **95%** of supply chains must quickly react to change, but **only 7%** can execute decisions in real time.
    59|
    60|Without real-time execution, organizations know what to do but cannot act fast enough. **Digital supply chain investment in real-time decision execution will increase 5x by 2028.**
    61|
    62|**Three-step transformation:**
    63|1. Access to real-time data from across the end-to-end supply chain
    64|2. Capability to analyze that data on the fly, enabling real-time decision making
    65|3. Real-time decision making that triggers an expectation for real-time execution
    66|
    67|**Enablers:** Overcome legacy technology and data gaps by deploying AI, large language models (LLMs), augmented reality (AR)/virtual reality (VR), and other technologies that enable rather than overwhelm.
    68|
    69|### 3. CX for Supply Chain
    70|Improving customer experience (CX) is a **top 3 business priority**. Supply chain performance is a key driver of repurchase behavior.
    71|
    72|> **Enabled customers are 2x as likely to repurchase**, but **only 23%** of supply chains focus on enabling their customers.
    73|
    74|Many organizations prioritize operational efficiency and OTIF (on time, in full) over reducing customer pain points.
    75|
    76|**Key actions to scale CX globally:**
    77|- Gather **voice of the customer (VoC)** data to inform personas and journey maps that reduce customer and employee effort
    78|- **Streamline the supply chain service menu**, offering capabilities "**as a service**" and differentiating the experience order-to-order across the buying journey
    79|- Gain funding via **cost-to-serve transparency** to address high service delivery costs
    80|- Accelerate the pace of launching new services
    81|
    82|### 4. Human-Centric Work & EVP
    83|> Over the next three to five years, **63% of supply chain organizations** expect the high competition for talent to continue.
    84|
    85|Only a minority anticipate increased talent availability from historically underrepresented regions (Africa, Middle East, Eastern Europe) or from AI deployments alone.
    86|
    87|To attract, retain, and engage future-ready talent, CSCOs must offer an **Employee Value Proposition (EVP)** that moves beyond financial motivation:
    88|- Flexible, purposeful day-to-day experiences
    89|- Environments where employees feel understood, autonomous, valued, invested, and cared for
    90|- Pathways for personal development and **choice in work**
    91|
    92|**Competitive advantage** stems from attracting the right skills *and* maintaining higher employee engagement to drive organizational success.
    93|
    94|### 5. Sustainable Supply Chain
    95|> **67% of supply chains** have formal objectives to increase sustainability, but many struggle to translate goals into operational reality.
    96|
    97|Sustainability must be embedded into network design, sourcing decisions, and product lifecycle management rather than treated as a standalone initiative.
