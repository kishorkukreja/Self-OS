    10|# Top 8 Omnichannel Logistics Challenges
    11|
    12|**Source:** [Prologis](https://www.prologis.com/what-we-do/resources/omnichannel-logistics-challenges)
    13|
    14|---
    15|
    16|## Consumer Expectations & Definition
    17|
    18|Modern consumers demand **instant gratification**—easy, quick access to products both in-store and online, with expedited delivery, often same-day.
    19|
    20|**Omnichannel logistics** synchronizes inventory, logistics, and distribution across sales channels to meet consumer demand. It involves every supply chain facet—**retailer, manufacturer, distributor, and wholesaler**—to optimize sales and shipments.
    21|
    22|> **Example:** A customer orders a product from your website. Your distribution center is hundreds of miles away, but you have a retail store in the customer’s hometown that stocks the product. Instead of shipping from the distribution center, it’s packaged and shipped from the nearby store. Your inventory is updated across all channels, and the customer receives the order quickly.
    23|

    26|## The 8 Omnichannel Supply Chain Challenges
    27|
    28|### 1. Lack of Inventory Visibility
    29|When product is "blowing out the doors" (as during holidays), tracking inventory across multiple distribution centers and retail locations is difficult. Promising next-day delivery requires:
    30|- Efficient order-fulfillment systems
    31|- Accurate inventory visibility
    32|- The right metrics to track **efficiency and cost effectiveness** as product flows through the distribution network
    33|
    34|### 2. Lack of Inventory Transit Visibility
    35|Shippers often cannot track progress from the distribution center to the customer until the shipment reaches the post office or the doorstep. Critical improvements include:
    36|- Better **data and communication exchange**
    37|- **Machine learning** to improve predictive capabilities and predict accurate delivery times
    38|
    39|### 3. Disconnected Supply Chain Processes
    40|When warehouses and distribution centers operate on different systems, communication breaks down. Like a real chain, **every link must be connected** for the whole string to be strong.
    41|
    42|### 4. Inefficient Order Processing
    43|Next-day delivery promises require the order fulfillment process to be **reliable and efficient**.
    44|
    45|### 5. The Wrong Shipping Solution
    46|Multiple shipping options exist, but they are not interchangeable. Avoid relying on just one shipping solution; **use the right one for the right customer**.
    47|
    48|### 6. Inefficient Return Logistics
    49|If a business cannot provide easy returns, it will likely lose future orders. In an omnichannel landscape, **customers should be able to return online orders to brick-and-mortar stores**.
    50|
    51|### 7. Manual Inventory Management Processes
    52|Manual, paper-based processes are more mistake-prone than automated ones. Required automation includes:
    53|- **RFID tags**
    54|- **Barcode scanning**
    55|- Other advanced systems
    56|
    57|### 8. The Wrong 3PL
    58|Partnering with the right **third-party logistics provider** is critical for inventory optimization and omnichannel fulfillment. A competent 3PL:
    59|- Understands which **metrics to use and how to measure them**
    60|- Drives **continuous improvement**
    61|- Is an essential part of a successful, smooth-flowing supply chain
    62|

    64|
    65|## Solutions to Improve the Customer Experience
    66|
    67|Good omnichannel supply chain management provides a **seamless, positive shopping experience** across brick-and-mortar and e-commerce channels. To avoid the challenges above:
    68|
    69|- **Partner with the right 3PL provider**
    70|- **Leverage technology** to achieve accuracy, speed, and convenience
