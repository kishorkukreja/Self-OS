# Supply Chain Signals — 2026-W18 PPT / consulting-style outline

Recommended deck style: 6-slide executive story. Use a "Midnight Executive" palette: navy title/conclusion slides, off-white content slides, amber constraint accents. Every slide should have a visual element: dashboards, route map, process flow, or chain schematic. No plain bullet slides.

## Slide 1 — Executive takeaway: supply-chain disruption is now a cash decision

- **Message:** This week’s signals point to working capital, margin, and capex exposure, not just service disruption.
- **Visual:** Five-node pressure map: energy chokepoint, manufacturing input costs, customs refunds, port dwell, AI infrastructure.
- **Speaker note:** Frame resilience as decision quality under constraint.

## Slide 2 — Situation: growth indicators are masking cost and lead-time stress

- **Message:** Manufacturing remains in expansion, but the operating inputs are deteriorating.
- **Evidence:** ISM PMI 52.7%; Prices 84.6%; Supplier Deliveries 60.6%; Employment 46.4%.
- **Visual:** KPI dashboard with green top-line PMI and amber/red sub-indicators.
- **Source:** ISM; Reuters.

## Slide 3 — Bottleneck: Hormuz has shifted from event to network redesign

- **Message:** Route diversions and port dwell show a new logistics baseline, not a temporary exception.
- **Evidence:** 34,294 diversions; Navi Mumbai dwell at 23.47 days; transshipment volume up more than 700% vs February daily averages.
- **Visual:** Route map with overflow nodes and dwell callouts.
- **Source:** project44; Reuters Graphics.

## Slide 4 — Business impact: averages are creating bad decisions

- **Message:** Average lead time, inventory, landed cost, and service level obscure the real decision unit.
- **Visual:** 2x2 matrix: lead time → lane; inventory → SKU; landed cost → entry/customer; service → customer promise.
- **Operator questions:** Which lane assumptions are stale? Which inventory is strategic? Which customs entries block cash? Which customers are protected by unrepeatable heroics?

## Slide 5 — Strategic chain: Wafer to Rack

- **Message:** AI infrastructure depends on synchronized physical capacity, not only chip allocation.
- **Visual:** End-to-end chain schematic: wafer → HBM/packaging/test → optical/network/power/cooling → rack integration → data center/grid/commissioning.
- **Evidence:** Reuters reporting on China chip supply-chain strain, European electrification/data-center demand, and U.S.-Philippines allied manufacturing hub.

## Slide 6 — Operator moves and watchlist

- **Message:** Build decision-grade supply-chain control loops.
- **Moves:** Clean customs/refund data; segment inventory buffers by named constraint; update lane-level routing baselines; connect visibility alerts to margin/service decisions; reserve scarce Wafer-to-Rack capacity early.
- **Watchlist:** May 8 UP/NS filing comments; May 11 expected CAPE refund; May 12 GEP/S&P volatility index; Asian port dwell; HBM and transformer lead times.
- **Visual:** Action roadmap with owner lanes: finance, trade compliance, planning, logistics, procurement, executive capex.
