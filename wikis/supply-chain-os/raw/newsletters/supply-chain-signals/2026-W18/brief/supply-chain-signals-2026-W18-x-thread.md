# Supply Chain Signals — 2026-W18 X thread

1/ This week’s supply-chain story is cash, not chaos.

Hormuz, tariffs, slower supplier deliveries, port dwell, AI infrastructure constraints. Familiar headlines. New pressure point: every one of them now hits capital allocation.

2/ U.S. manufacturing stayed in expansion: ISM PMI 52.7%.

But Prices hit 84.6%, the highest since April 2022. Supplier Deliveries slowed. Employment contracted.

Demand is not the only question. Serving demand profitably is the question.

3/ Canada showed the inventory trap.

PMI jumped to 53.3, but S&P Global warned the lift looked like war-related stock building, not clean demand.

Inventory built from fear can protect service. It can also trap cash in the wrong node.

4/ Hormuz is no longer just a disruption headline.

project44 counted 34,294 route diversions in four weeks. Navi Mumbai import dwell rose to 23.47 days. Transshipment volume there jumped more than 700% vs February daily averages.

That is network redesign.

5/ Tariff refunds are now a data-quality race.

Flexport says 63% of CAPE declarations passed initial validation by April 26. Nearly 16% of entries on validated declarations were then rejected.

Bad customs data delays cash.

6/ Chain of the Week: Wafer to Rack.

AI infrastructure is not just chips. It is wafers, HBM, packaging, optical modules, racks, power, cooling, transformers, permits, construction, and grid access.

The scarce unit is deployable compute.

7/ WTO projects merchandise trade growth slowing from 4.6% in 2025 to 1.9% in 2026.

But AI-related investment contributed almost half of 2025 merchandise trade growth.

Trade is splitting: AI capex goods up, ordinary goods under pressure.

8/ The problem beneath the headline: old averages are lying.

Average lead time hides lane variance. Average inventory hides nervous buying. Average landed cost hides surcharge leakage and customs rejects.

Operators need SKU, lane, supplier, and customer-level decisions.

9/ Watch next:
- May 8 UP/NS merger filing comments
- May 11 expected first CAPE refund
- May 12 GEP/S&P volatility index
- Navi Mumbai / Singapore / Shanghai dwell
- HBM, transformer, and data-center power constraints

10/ The useful capability is not "resilience" as a slogan.

It is decision quality under constraint: what to protect, reroute, fix, reserve, reprice, or explain before the miss happens.
