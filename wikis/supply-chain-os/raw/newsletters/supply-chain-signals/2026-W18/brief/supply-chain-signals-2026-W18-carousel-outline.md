# Supply Chain Signals — 2026-W18 carousel outline

Recommended visual direction: baoyu-infographic style, `dense-modules` or `bento-grid` layout, `technical-schematic` with restrained corporate color. Landscape LinkedIn document format. Use supply-chain nodes, cash-flow arrows, lane maps, and decision cards rather than generic globe imagery.

## Card 1 — This week’s signal: cash, not chaos

- **Core point:** Disruption headlines now translate directly into working capital, margin, and capex choices.
- **Suggested visual:** Bento grid of five pressure tiles: Hormuz, PMI prices, CAPE refunds, AI racks, port dwell. Each tile has a small cash-impact tag.
- **Source note:** Reuters; ISM; Flexport; project44; WTO.

## Card 2 — Manufacturing looks healthy until you read the cost line

- **Core point:** ISM PMI stayed at 52.7%, but Prices hit 84.6% and Supplier Deliveries slowed to 60.6%.
- **Suggested visual:** Dashboard card with three large numbers and a small warning strip: "Expansion with cost pressure."
- **Source note:** ISM April 2026 Manufacturing PMI; Reuters May 1.

## Card 3 — Inventory can be a fear response

- **Core point:** Canada’s PMI rose to 53.3, but S&P Global warned stock building was driven by worry, not clean demand.
- **Suggested visual:** Split module: "strategic buffer" vs "nervous buying" with cash trapped in the second column.
- **Source note:** Reuters May 1 Canada PMI.

## Card 4 — Hormuz is now a routing redesign

- **Core point:** project44 counted 34,294 diversions; Navi Mumbai dwell rose to 23.47 days; transshipment volumes rose more than 700%.
- **Suggested visual:** Technical map of Gulf bypass routes feeding India, Singapore, Shanghai. Highlight overflow nodes.
- **Source note:** project44; Reuters Graphics.

## Card 5 — Customs data is cash infrastructure

- **Core point:** CAPE refunds may begin around May 11, but entry-specific validation rejects are already a bottleneck.
- **Suggested visual:** Process flow: entry filing → validation → rejection/fix loop → refund → finance forecast.
- **Source note:** Flexport April 30.

## Card 6 — Wafer to Rack: the AI infrastructure chain

- **Core point:** Deployable compute requires chips plus power, cooling, networking, construction, permits, and grid access.
- **Suggested visual:** Linear-progression diagram from wafer → packaging/HBM → server/rack → data center → grid/commissioning.
- **Source note:** Reuters Semicon China; Reuters ABB/data-center demand; Reuters U.S.-Philippines hub.

## Card 7 — Old averages are lying

- **Core point:** Average lead time, inventory, landed cost, and service level hide where decisions actually need to happen.
- **Suggested visual:** Four cards showing "average" cracked open into lane, SKU, supplier, and customer views.
- **Source note:** Synthesis from ISM, project44, Flexport, Reuters.

## Card 8 — Watchlist

- **Core point:** Next signals: UP/NS merger comments, first CAPE refunds, GEP/S&P volatility index, Asian port dwell, HBM/power constraints.
- **Suggested visual:** Calendar/watchtower module with dates and icons.
- **Source note:** Supply Chain Dive; Flexport; S&P/GEP; project44; Reuters.
