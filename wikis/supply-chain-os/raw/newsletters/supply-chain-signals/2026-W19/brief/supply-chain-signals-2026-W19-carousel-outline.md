# Supply Chain Signals — 2026-W19 carousel outline

Recommended visual direction: baoyu-infographic style, `dense-modules` or `bento-grid` layout, `technical-schematic` style. Use a dark executive base with copper and electric-green accents. Format: LinkedIn document carousel, 8 cards, landscape 16:9 or square 1:1.

## Card 1 — The constraint keeps moving
- **Core point:** This week was not one disruption. It was constraint migration.
- **Suggested visual:** Central "live constraint map" node with orbiting constraints: material, duty, power, freight, capacity, supplier qualification, cash, customer promise.
- **Source note:** Weekly synthesis from ISM, Reuters, FAO, WTO, FreightWaves.

## Card 2 — Manufacturing: calm headline, ugly internals
- **Core point:** PMI at 52.7 says expansion; Prices at 84.6 and Supplier Deliveries at 60.6 say squeeze.
- **Suggested visual:** Three metric tiles with small arrows and short labels.
- **Source note:** ISM April Manufacturing PMI; Reuters manufacturing coverage.

## Card 3 — Tariffs become an operating calendar
- **Core point:** USTR's Section 301 probe and July deadline belong in sourcing, pricing, and inventory plans.
- **Suggested visual:** Timeline from hearing week to July decision window; small icons for customs, supplier qualification, price pass-through.
- **Source note:** Reuters, May 5, 2026.

## Card 4 — Energy shows up in food
- **Core point:** FAO Food Price Index rose to 130.7; vegetable oils were hit by energy and biofuels pressure.
- **Suggested visual:** Energy barrel feeding into fertilizer, biofuels, vegetable oils, transport, grocery shelf.
- **Source note:** FAO April 2026 release; Reuters May 8 story.

## Card 5 — Freight refuses one clean story
- **Core point:** Imports can be soft while fuel, routing, truck capacity, and secondary capacity keep costs sticky.
- **Suggested visual:** Split-screen: "soft import signal" vs "cost pressure signal" with lane/fuel icons.
- **Source note:** FreightWaves May 2026 State of the Industry, sponsored/gated caveat.

## Card 6 — Chain of the week: wafer to rack
- **Core point:** AI infrastructure is an industrial supply chain, not only a chip allocation problem.
- **Suggested visual:** Layered stack: wafers → HBM/packaging → rack build → power/cooling → site/grid → operations.
- **Source note:** WTO, Manufacturing Dive/Omdia, IEA/OECD critical minerals.

## Card 7 — The live constraint map
- **Core point:** A useful map names the bottleneck, decision, dollar/service exposure, owner, and action window.
- **Suggested visual:** Five-field operating card, not a generic heatmap.
- **Source note:** Editorial synthesis from weekly sources.

## Card 8 — Operator question
- **Core point:** If the constraint moved this morning, how long would your plan take to notice?
- **Suggested visual:** Clock + control room + moving bottleneck marker.
- **Source note:** Derived operator takeaway.
