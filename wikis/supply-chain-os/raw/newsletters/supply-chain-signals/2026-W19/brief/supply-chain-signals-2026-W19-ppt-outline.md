# Supply Chain Signals — 2026-W19 PPT / consulting-style outline

Recommended deck style: 7-slide executive story. Use a "Midnight Executive" palette with copper/green accents. Every slide should have a visual element: metric cards, systems map, timeline, or decision-flow diagram. Do not generate a PPTX unless explicitly requested.

## Slide 1 — Executive message: the constraint keeps moving
- **Headline:** Supply chains are not facing one disruption; they are facing a moving bottleneck.
- **Visual:** Live constraint map with material, duty, power, freight, capacity, supplier qualification, cash, customer promise.
- **Talk track:** This week’s sources show a rotating set of constraints. The operating model needs to identify the binding constraint fast enough to change the plan.

## Slide 2 — Situation: demand has not collapsed, but assumptions are degrading
- **Headline:** Manufacturing still expands while cost and delivery pressure worsen.
- **Visual:** Metric card row: PMI 52.7; Prices 84.6; Supplier Deliveries 60.6; Customers' Inventories 39.1.
- **Sources:** ISM; Reuters.
- **Consulting note:** Separate demand signal from risk buying and buffer rebuilding.

## Slide 3 — External pressure: tariff deadlines and energy pass-through
- **Headline:** Policy and energy are moving faster than planning calendars.
- **Visual:** Two-column timeline: USTR Section 301 July window; FAO food-price/energy linkage.
- **Sources:** Reuters; FAO.
- **Operator implication:** Duty rates, fuel, fertilizer, biofuels, and customer pricing need owners in S&OP.

## Slide 4 — Chain of the week: wafer to rack
- **Headline:** AI infrastructure is an industrial supply chain.
- **Visual:** Layered dependency stack from wafer fab to data-center operations.
- **Sources:** WTO; Manufacturing Dive/Omdia; IEA/OECD.
- **Operator implication:** The bottleneck can be power, HBM, packaging, transformers, cooling, or site readiness, not only GPU allocation.

## Slide 5 — Bottleneck economics: visibility without decision rights is weak
- **Headline:** Dashboards do not fix constraints; decisions do.
- **Visual:** Decision-flow: detect risk → quantify exposure → rank actions → assign owner → execute → update plan.
- **Source:** FourKites release as vendor example; editorial synthesis.
- **Operator implication:** Measure whether the system changes allocation, inventory target, customer promise, freight booking, or price.

## Slide 6 — Recommended management artifact: live constraint map
- **Headline:** Add one operating object between S&OP, procurement, logistics, finance, and sales.
- **Visual:** Template card with fields: binding constraint; affected SKUs/customers/sites; exposure; available actions; owner; decision deadline.
- **Operator implication:** Use weekly in normal cadence and daily during shocks.

## Slide 7 — Watchlist and next actions
- **Headline:** Watch the assumptions most likely to expire.
- **Visual:** 2x3 watchlist grid: tariffs, PMI prices/deliveries, food/energy, freight/fuel, Wafer-to-Rack power/HBM, AI planning execution.
- **Close:** If the constraint moved this morning, how long would your plan take to notice?
