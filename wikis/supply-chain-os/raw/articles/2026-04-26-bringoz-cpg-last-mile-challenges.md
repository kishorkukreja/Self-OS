# How CPG Brands are Overcoming Last-Mile Delivery Challenges

**Source:** [Bringoz](https://www.bringoz.com/how-cpg-brands-are-overcoming-last-mile-delivery-challenges/)  
**Published:** September 23, 2025  
**Reading Time:** ~3 min

---

## Overview

For consumer packaged goods (CPG) companies, last-mile delivery is typically the **most complex and expensive leg of the supply chain**. Whether stocking brick-and-mortar shelves, fulfilling direct-to-consumer subscriptions, or meeting eCommerce delivery windows, the final leg significantly impacts both **cost and customer experience**. With rising consumer expectations for speed and convenience, CPG brands must rethink their approach to this critical stage.

---

## 1. Build Flexible Delivery Networks

CPG companies are constructing agile delivery ecosystems using a mix of:
- **In-house drivers**
- **Independent contractors**
- **Third-party logistics (3PL) providers**

### Benefits of Flexible Fleets
- **Agility:** Easily adapt to delivery delays and demand fluctuations
- **Customer Experience Control:** Managing deliveries in-house allows brands to own service quality and optimize resource allocation
- **Scalability:** Partnering with 3PLs or specialized carriers provides access to established networks, technology, and infrastructure

### Market Expansion
Local carriers bring the experience and resources needed to help CPG companies **expand faster into new markets** while minimizing costs and maintaining last-mile flexibility.

---

## 2. Invest in Warehouse Automation

To accelerate order fulfillment, brands are investing heavily in **urban-centric warehouse automation**.

### Key Advantages
- Process orders more quickly
- Increase operational efficiency
- Overcome **labor shortages**

### Case Study: Unilever
> **Unilever** is prioritizing increasing warehouse automation within its ice cream business.
>
> Highly automated **"dark" warehouses** using technology like **drones for inventory management** will become increasingly important in cold chain logistics, particularly to offset recruiting challenges, according to **Sandeep Desai**, EVP and Chief Product Supply Chain Officer for the company’s ice cream business.

**Key Statistic:** Within Unilever’s ice cream business, about **10% of its warehouses are currently dark**, and the company aims to grow that percentage in the next several years.

---

## 3. Route Optimization and Real-Time Visibility

### The Problem
Many CPG companies suffer from:
- Lack of **real-time visibility**
- Weak route optimization
- No data insights to evaluate performance
- **Static routing and manual route planning**, leading to time-consuming tasks and operational inefficiencies

### The Solution
**Delivery management technologies** automate and streamline operations while improving productivity and transparency.

#### Advanced Route Optimization
Algorithms consider multiple factors simultaneously:
- Traffic conditions
- Delivery time-windows
- Driver schedules
- Vehicle capacity

> *"Advanced route optimization algorithms can consider various factors including traffic, delivery time-windows, driver schedules, and vehicle capacity to create the most efficient routes."*

#### Real-Time Adjustments
- Route optimization enables managers to **adjust routes on the fly** in response to traffic, cancellations, or last-minute orders
- This minimizes delivery delays while **maximizing resource utilization**

#### Visibility & Customer Experience
- **Delivery management platforms** provide a real-time view of the entire last-mile operation
- **Real-time tracking tools** give customers visibility into order location and ETA, helping to reduce failed deliveries and improve satisfaction
- **Data analytics and reporting** provide actionable insights to improve delivery performance, optimize resources, and enhance customer experiences

---

## Strategic Outlook

As sales channels fragment across **retail stores, eCommerce websites, and marketplaces**, the last mile has become an increasingly crowded space.

**Winning Formula:** Brands that combine a **smarter delivery network** with **flexible capacity** and **real-time delivery orchestration** will be best positioned to meet last-mile expectations and growing demand in the years ahead.
