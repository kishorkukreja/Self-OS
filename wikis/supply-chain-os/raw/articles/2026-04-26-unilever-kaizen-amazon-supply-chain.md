    10|# Unilever Strengthens Supply Chain with Amazon via Kaizen Collaboration
    11|
    12|**Published:** 9 January 2025  
    13|**Source:** Unilever News
    14|
    15|---
    16|
    17|## Overview
    18|
    19|Unilever has been working with Amazon—one of its most valued e-commerce retailers—to streamline operations for shipping products to Amazon fulfilment centres, ensuring brands are readily available on Amazon.com.
    20|
    21|> "Maintaining strong relationships with retailers like Amazon, which has built new technology-enabled business models to serve changing customer habits, is vital to ensure our brands are well presented to our consumers and available for purchase at all times."
    22|> 
    23|> **— Mark Sumner**, Unilever Global Digital Commerce Customer Supply Chain Director
    24|
    25|---
    26|
    27|## Strategic Context
    28|
    29|- **E-commerce priority:** Amazon remains essential to Unilever's growth plans, requiring a fundamentally different approach than traditional brick-and-mortar retail due to vast stock ranges, fast delivery demands, and fully digital operations.
    30|- **Supply chain pedigree:** Both companies are recognized as two of only four **Supply Chain Masters** in Gartner's prestigious global rankings for sustained performance and excellence.
    31|
    32|---
    33|
    34|## The Kaizen Approach
    35|
    36|Since **2022**, Unilever has applied **Kaizen**—a Japanese methodology for continuous improvement that challenges conventional assumptions—to its Amazon partnership.
    37|
    38|### How It Works
    39|- Cross-functional teams from both companies meet in offices, warehouses, and fulfilment centres worldwide
    40|- End-to-end supply chain pain points are mapped collaboratively
    41|- Data-driven analysis reduces waste and inefficiency in order-to-fulfilment operations
    42|- Teams align on actions to streamline and integrate operations, building stronger local relationships
    43|
    44|### Scale
    45|- **150+** supply chain and business leaders engaged
    46|- Workshops conducted across **six global regions**
    47|
    48|> "To date, we’ve carried out Kaizen workshops with Amazon in **Australia, Brazil, Europe, Egypt, India and the US**. Through a series of very open, productive conversations and actions, we have improved **15 key performance indicators** and solved **over 100 pain points** since we started this programme in July 2022."
    49|>
    50|> **— Mark Sumner**
    51|
    52|---
    53|
    54|## Key Results by Region
    55|
    56|| Region / Year | Outcome |
    57||---------------|---------|
    58|| **Australia (2022)** | **40% reduction** in Unilever’s out-of-stock rate on Amazon |
    59|| **Egypt (2023)** | **20% improvement** in order acceptance rate |
    60|| **India (2024)** | **35% increase** in order fill rate; **55% reduction** in defects delivering to Amazon fulfilment centres |
    61|
    62|### Overall Business Impact
    63|- Lower supply chain costs for Unilever
    64|- Faster delivery speeds
    65|- More customer-centric brand portfolio
    66|- More robust packaging
    67|- Higher in-stock rates, improving the Amazon customer experience
    68|
    69|> "These results help deliver lower supply chain costs for Unilever, faster delivery speeds, a more customer-centric portfolio of brands, more robust packaging and higher in-stock rates, which improves the experience for Amazon customers."
    70|>
    71|> **— Mark Sumner**
    72|
    73|---
    74|
    75|## Operational Improvements
    76|
    77|### Major Change
    78|Unilever created a **new internal dashboard** to share supply chain performance data across global teams. This has:
    79|- Improved demand planning
    80|- Boosted in-stock availability for Amazon customers
    81|- Achieved greater alignment on supply chain strategy
    82|
    83|### Small Change, Big Impact
    84|Shipping packaging was modified for some products so Amazon no longer needs to re-pack them before sending to shoppers—they simply use Unilever's box and add an address label.
    85|
    86|---
    87|
    88|## Bottom Line
    89|
    90|Through biannual Kaizen workshops since July 2022, Unilever and Amazon have built a stronger, future-fit supply chain by combining data-driven problem solving with closer operational integration—delivering measurable improvements in availability, cost efficiency, and customer experience across six continents.
