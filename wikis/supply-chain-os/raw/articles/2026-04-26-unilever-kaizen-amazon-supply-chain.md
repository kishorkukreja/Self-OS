# Unilever Strengthens Supply Chain with Amazon via Kaizen Collaboration

**Published:** 9 January 2025  
**Source:** Unilever News

---

## Overview

Unilever has been working with Amazon—one of its most valued e-commerce retailers—to streamline operations for shipping products to Amazon fulfilment centres, ensuring brands are readily available on Amazon.com.

> "Maintaining strong relationships with retailers like Amazon, which has built new technology-enabled business models to serve changing customer habits, is vital to ensure our brands are well presented to our consumers and available for purchase at all times."
> 
> **— Mark Sumner**, Unilever Global Digital Commerce Customer Supply Chain Director

---

## Strategic Context

- **E-commerce priority:** Amazon remains essential to Unilever's growth plans, requiring a fundamentally different approach than traditional brick-and-mortar retail due to vast stock ranges, fast delivery demands, and fully digital operations.
- **Supply chain pedigree:** Both companies are recognized as two of only four **Supply Chain Masters** in Gartner's prestigious global rankings for sustained performance and excellence.

---

## The Kaizen Approach

Since **2022**, Unilever has applied **Kaizen**—a Japanese methodology for continuous improvement that challenges conventional assumptions—to its Amazon partnership.

### How It Works
- Cross-functional teams from both companies meet in offices, warehouses, and fulfilment centres worldwide
- End-to-end supply chain pain points are mapped collaboratively
- Data-driven analysis reduces waste and inefficiency in order-to-fulfilment operations
- Teams align on actions to streamline and integrate operations, building stronger local relationships

### Scale
- **150+** supply chain and business leaders engaged
- Workshops conducted across **six global regions**

> "To date, we’ve carried out Kaizen workshops with Amazon in **Australia, Brazil, Europe, Egypt, India and the US**. Through a series of very open, productive conversations and actions, we have improved **15 key performance indicators** and solved **over 100 pain points** since we started this programme in July 2022."
>
> **— Mark Sumner**

---

## Key Results by Region

| Region / Year | Outcome |
|---------------|---------|
| **Australia (2022)** | **40% reduction** in Unilever’s out-of-stock rate on Amazon |
| **Egypt (2023)** | **20% improvement** in order acceptance rate |
| **India (2024)** | **35% increase** in order fill rate; **55% reduction** in defects delivering to Amazon fulfilment centres |

### Overall Business Impact
- Lower supply chain costs for Unilever
- Faster delivery speeds
- More customer-centric brand portfolio
- More robust packaging
- Higher in-stock rates, improving the Amazon customer experience

> "These results help deliver lower supply chain costs for Unilever, faster delivery speeds, a more customer-centric portfolio of brands, more robust packaging and higher in-stock rates, which improves the experience for Amazon customers."
>
> **— Mark Sumner**

---

## Operational Improvements

### Major Change
Unilever created a **new internal dashboard** to share supply chain performance data across global teams. This has:
- Improved demand planning
- Boosted in-stock availability for Amazon customers
- Achieved greater alignment on supply chain strategy

### Small Change, Big Impact
Shipping packaging was modified for some products so Amazon no longer needs to re-pack them before sending to shoppers—they simply use Unilever's box and add an address label.

---

## Bottom Line

Through biannual Kaizen workshops since July 2022, Unilever and Amazon have built a stronger, future-fit supply chain by combining data-driven problem solving with closer operational integration—delivering measurable improvements in availability, cost efficiency, and customer experience across six continents.
