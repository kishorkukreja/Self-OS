    10|# 5 CPG Industry Trends to Watch in 2026
    11|
    12|**Source:** [DOSS.com](https://www.doss.com/blog/5-cpg-industry-trends-to-watch-in-2026)  
    13|**Author:** Kathryn Nassar  
    14|**Date:** February 2, 2026
    15|
    16|---
    17|
    18|## Executive Summary
    19|
    20|The CPG industry is undergoing a fundamental operational transformation rather than just marketing innovation. **Nearly half of CPG executives report their current business structure won't hold up for another decade**, signaling urgent need for operational transformation. The companies that master AI-driven forecasting, autonomous systems, collaborative robotics, sustainable packaging compliance, and distributed fulfillment will build durable competitive advantages.
    21|
    22|---
    23|
    24|## 1. AI-Powered Demand Forecasting & Supply Chain Resilience
    25|
    26|AI is replacing historical sales data and intuition as the foundation of demand planning.
    27|
    28|- **71% of CPG industry leaders** have integrated AI into at least one business function, with demand forecasting leading the charge (*2025 McKinsey survey*).
    29|- **64% of respondents** reported increased supply chain challenges year over year—including geopolitical instability, labor constraints, and evolving consumer expectations (*NVIDIA 2026 State of AI in Retail and CPG survey*).
    30|- **51%** identified supply chain operational efficiency and throughput as their **top priority** for AI implementation.
    31|
    32|**Operational implications:**
    33|- Smart inventory systems now determine optimal stock locations, position products closer to demand centers, and predict equipment maintenance needs based on production schedules.
    34|- Real-time supply chain visibility platforms have become essential for end-to-end tracking of products, components, and raw materials.
    35|- Operations teams are moving away from single-sourcing toward **multi-source supply networks** with qualified backup suppliers managed through digital systems.
    36|
    37|---
    38|
    39|## 2. Agentic AI and Intelligent Automation
    40|
    41|2026 marks the shift from recommendation-based AI to autonomous, decision-making systems.
    42|
    43|- **47%** of retail and CPG companies are using or assessing agentic AI.
    44|- **20%** report that AI agents are already active in their organizations.
    45|
    46|**Key use cases for operations:**
    47|- Autonomous reordering of raw materials when inventory falls below optimal levels.
    48|- Supplier pricing negotiations based on real-time market conditions.
    49|- Dynamic production schedule adjustments in response to demand signals.
    50|- Real-time inventory rebalancing and dynamic pricing at scale.
    51|
    52|**Goals for agentic AI** (*NVIDIA survey*):
    53|- **57%** — Increased process speed and efficiency
    54|- **40%** — Enhanced customer experience and personalization
    55|- **40%** — Improved decision-making with real-time data
    56|
    57|> **Insight:** Despite AI's potential, nearly **60% of CPG executives** are prioritizing AI primarily for cost improvement (forecasting, back-office automation), treating it as an efficiency tool rather than a transformative technology. Leaders who leverage AI to build smarter product pipelines, reshape consumer engagement, and unlock new revenue models will gain the most significant advantage.
    58|

    60|
    61|## 3. Robotics and Collaborative Automation in Production
    62|
    63|Labor shortages and rising wages are accelerating automation investments, but the deployment model is changing.
    64|
    65|- **63%** of respondents believe robotics has the potential to **disrupt or create competitive advantage**—the highest among all technologies surveyed (*2025 MHI Annual Industry Report*).
    66|- **83%** predict adoption within the next five years.
    67|- Perceived impact of robotics grew **11% year-over-year** (vs. AI's 1% increase), despite only a modest 0.5% increase in actual robot sales—indicating strong intent to invest despite economic uncertainty.
    68|
    69|**The Cobot Shift:**
    70|- Unlike previous automation waves, **collaborative robots (cobots)** work alongside humans, enhancing capabilities rather than eliminating jobs.
    71|- Cobots handle repetitive, physically demanding tasks (palletizing, sorting, packaging), while human workers focus on quality control, problem-solving, and process optimization.
    72|- Physical AI systems are enhancing **flexibility and throughput** in response to workforce pressures.
    73|- Computer vision systems for quality inspection can check thousands of products per hour, catching subtle defects that signal upstream production problems.
    74|
    75|**Workforce strategy:** Leading CPG companies are retraining line workers to operate and maintain automated systems, creating higher-skilled, higher-paying roles while maintaining the flexibility to adjust production runs quickly.
    76|

    79|## 4. Sustainable Packaging and Circular Economy Operations
    80|
    81|Sustainability has shifted from marketing initiative to **operational and legal imperative**.
    82|
    83|**Regulatory Landscape:**
    84|- **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)** data reporting began in 2025 in **Oregon, Colorado, Minnesota, and California**.
    85|- **Seven states** have enacted packaging EPR laws; **eleven additional states** have introduced similar legislation.
    86|- **California SB 343** compliance deadline approaching.
    87|
    88|**Operational Impact:**
    89|- CPG companies must now track packaging materials, recyclability, and end-of-life disposition with the same rigor as inventory and logistics.
    90|- Sustainable packaging is becoming a **cost optimization** opportunity through material reduction and reusable packaging systems.
    91|- Reverse logistics networks must handle returns, recycling, and refurbishment at scale.
