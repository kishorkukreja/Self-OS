# 5 CPG Industry Trends to Watch in 2026

**Source:** [DOSS.com](https://www.doss.com/blog/5-cpg-industry-trends-to-watch-in-2026)  
**Author:** Kathryn Nassar  
**Date:** February 2, 2026

---

## Executive Summary

The CPG industry is undergoing a fundamental operational transformation rather than just marketing innovation. **Nearly half of CPG executives report their current business structure won't hold up for another decade**, signaling urgent need for operational transformation. The companies that master AI-driven forecasting, autonomous systems, collaborative robotics, sustainable packaging compliance, and distributed fulfillment will build durable competitive advantages.

---

## 1. AI-Powered Demand Forecasting & Supply Chain Resilience

AI is replacing historical sales data and intuition as the foundation of demand planning.

- **71% of CPG industry leaders** have integrated AI into at least one business function, with demand forecasting leading the charge (*2025 McKinsey survey*).
- **64% of respondents** reported increased supply chain challenges year over year—including geopolitical instability, labor constraints, and evolving consumer expectations (*NVIDIA 2026 State of AI in Retail and CPG survey*).
- **51%** identified supply chain operational efficiency and throughput as their **top priority** for AI implementation.

**Operational implications:**
- Smart inventory systems now determine optimal stock locations, position products closer to demand centers, and predict equipment maintenance needs based on production schedules.
- Real-time supply chain visibility platforms have become essential for end-to-end tracking of products, components, and raw materials.
- Operations teams are moving away from single-sourcing toward **multi-source supply networks** with qualified backup suppliers managed through digital systems.

---

## 2. Agentic AI and Intelligent Automation

2026 marks the shift from recommendation-based AI to autonomous, decision-making systems.

- **47%** of retail and CPG companies are using or assessing agentic AI.
- **20%** report that AI agents are already active in their organizations.

**Key use cases for operations:**
- Autonomous reordering of raw materials when inventory falls below optimal levels.
- Supplier pricing negotiations based on real-time market conditions.
- Dynamic production schedule adjustments in response to demand signals.
- Real-time inventory rebalancing and dynamic pricing at scale.

**Goals for agentic AI** (*NVIDIA survey*):
- **57%** — Increased process speed and efficiency
- **40%** — Enhanced customer experience and personalization
- **40%** — Improved decision-making with real-time data

> **Insight:** Despite AI's potential, nearly **60% of CPG executives** are prioritizing AI primarily for cost improvement (forecasting, back-office automation), treating it as an efficiency tool rather than a transformative technology. Leaders who leverage AI to build smarter product pipelines, reshape consumer engagement, and unlock new revenue models will gain the most significant advantage.

---

## 3. Robotics and Collaborative Automation in Production

Labor shortages and rising wages are accelerating automation investments, but the deployment model is changing.

- **63%** of respondents believe robotics has the potential to **disrupt or create competitive advantage**—the highest among all technologies surveyed (*2025 MHI Annual Industry Report*).
- **83%** predict adoption within the next five years.
- Perceived impact of robotics grew **11% year-over-year** (vs. AI's 1% increase), despite only a modest 0.5% increase in actual robot sales—indicating strong intent to invest despite economic uncertainty.

**The Cobot Shift:**
- Unlike previous automation waves, **collaborative robots (cobots)** work alongside humans, enhancing capabilities rather than eliminating jobs.
- Cobots handle repetitive, physically demanding tasks (palletizing, sorting, packaging), while human workers focus on quality control, problem-solving, and process optimization.
- Physical AI systems are enhancing **flexibility and throughput** in response to workforce pressures.
- Computer vision systems for quality inspection can check thousands of products per hour, catching subtle defects that signal upstream production problems.

**Workforce strategy:** Leading CPG companies are retraining line workers to operate and maintain automated systems, creating higher-skilled, higher-paying roles while maintaining the flexibility to adjust production runs quickly.

---

## 4. Sustainable Packaging and Circular Economy Operations

Sustainability has shifted from marketing initiative to **operational and legal imperative**.

**Regulatory Landscape:**
- **Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR)** data reporting began in 2025 in **Oregon, Colorado, Minnesota, and California**.
- **Seven states** have enacted packaging EPR laws; **eleven additional states** have introduced similar legislation.
- **California SB 343** compliance deadline approaching.

**Operational Impact:**
- CPG companies must now track packaging materials, recyclability, and end-of-life disposition with the same rigor as inventory and logistics.
- Sustainable packaging is becoming a **cost optimization** opportunity through material reduction and reusable packaging systems.
- Reverse logistics networks must handle returns, recycling, and refurbishment at scale.
