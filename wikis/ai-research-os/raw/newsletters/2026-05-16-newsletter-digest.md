## 🔑 Top Insights

1. **Robotics is entering the "app store" era.** Unitree launched UniStore, the world's first app store for humanoid robots. G1 owners can download skill packages (dance, martial arts, walking styles). This shifts competitive moats from hardware specs to developer ecosystems — a strategic pivot that mirrors the smartphone transition.

2. **Humanoid robots are proving reliable at scale.** Figure AI's Helix-02 humanoid ran a 24-hour warehouse sorting livestream, processing 28K+ packages and pulling 3M+ social views. Originally an 8-hour test, it ran long — validating operational endurance in real logistics environments.

3. **Compute quality compounds into differentiated outcomes over time.** Lambda's CCO argues that data center tier (2 vs 3), physical build quality, and operational reliability create massive differentiation on 3-5 year contracts. Teams treating GPUs as interchangeable pay an increasing penalty in uptime and latency.

4. **AI leadership ≠ AI usage.** Lauren Vriens (former Accenture, scaled first enterprise GenAI product to $340M) distinguishes between using AI and explaining to executives how AI changes the revenue model — the latter is the high-value skill in any organisation.

5. **"Superintelligence for all" as a democratising frame.** Lambda positions itself as making combined human + AI intelligence accessible across domains — not locked inside one lab. The CCO predicts personal robot ownership (1X Neo rumoured at ~$20K) arriving soon.

6. **Robotics investment is accelerating fast.** Mind Robotics (chaired by Rivian CEO RJ Scaringe) hit $3B valuation after raising $400M. Humanoid (UK startup) deploying 2,000 robots in Schaeffler factories. Rocket Lab acquired Motiv Space Systems for space robotics vertical integration.

7. **Regulatory accountability is arriving for autonomous vehicles.** Waymo recalled ~3,800 robotaxis over flooding incidents; Avride is under federal investigation for 16 crashes in Texas. The "trust us" phase of self-driving is over.

8. **Personal robot cost compression is accelerating.** A university student cloned Disney's BD-X bipedal robot for under $3K using a lawn mower battery and NVIDIA simulation training — validating rapid cost compression in robotics hardware.
