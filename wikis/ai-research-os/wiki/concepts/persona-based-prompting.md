# Persona-Based Prompting

**Definition:** A prompting technique where the user defines a specific role, identity, or professional viewpoint for the LLM, causing it to adopt corresponding tone, reasoning patterns, and output conventions.

**Why it matters:** Persona-based prompting turns a general-purpose model into a domain specialist without fine-tuning. By anchoring the model to a recognisable professional identity — such as senior marketer, patient study partner, or skeptical research assistant — the user gains more consistent formatting, appropriate jargon levels, and task-relevant framing.
