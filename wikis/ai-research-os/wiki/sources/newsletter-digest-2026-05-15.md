## Summary

The May 15 newsletter digest clusters around ambient coding agents, agent economics, and the operational maturity of AI tooling. Codex mobile and Grok Build show developer agents moving beyond the local terminal into always-on control surfaces, while Anthropic’s agent-credit changes expose the cost side of that shift. The digest also records a mainstream backlash against uncritical agentic coding: supervision quality, debugging skill atrophy, and orchestration failure are becoming central concerns rather than fringe objections.

Several entries point to maturing infrastructure around agents and models. Opik’s production-failure-to-test-suite loop highlights evaluation as a live operations practice, not a one-time benchmark. Claude prompt-cache warmup, Qwen compression, Nous pretraining efficiency, and xAI’s open-sourced ranking stack show optimization pressure across inference, training, and deployment. For Self-OS, the digest is valuable as a daily market map: coding agents are becoming pervasive, but durable supervision, evaluation traces, and explicit review gates remain the safety layer that determines whether they improve work or erode operator skill.

## Key contributions
- Ambient coding-agent interfaces are spreading to mobile, CLI, and parallel subagent workflows.
- Agent cost allocation and skill-atropy concerns are becoming operational constraints.
- Evaluation harnesses and production-trace reuse are emerging as core agent infrastructure.
